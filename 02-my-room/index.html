Hey Ahmet, hey Efe 👋

Great work on your Explorer Profile! Now we go one step further.

Today you’re going to describe your room — and learn one of the most important speaking skills: how to make your sentences longer. A short sentence is a first step. A longer sentence feels more like real English.

The secret? Don’t wait. Just speak. If a word is missing, use a simple word, point to the idea, or make a funny guess. In this class, a wrong sentence is okay — a silent student is the only real problem.

Let’s make our sentences grow 🌱
★ TASK A

Grow Your Sentence

Watch how a small sentence grows into a bigger one. Each time, we add one more idea.

MODEL — WATCH IT GROW
There is a bed.
There is a big bed.
There is a big bed, and it is next to the window.
There is a big bed, and it is next to the window, but it is very old.

See? Each sentence gets longer by adding and, but, and a place word.

🌟 Bonus for brave Explorers: add something funny that is not true!
There is a big bed, and a small dragon is sleeping on it! 🐉

★ TASK C

“and” or “but”?

Two tiny words can change the whole sentence.
and = adding more  ·  but = a surprise / opposite idea

MODEL
My room is small and my desk is small too.
My room is small, but I love it.
My chair is old and broken.
The book is big, but the notebook is tiny.

THE NUVORI WAY

We don’t wait — we speak.

In speaking, the biggest mistake is silence. A longer sentence with a small mistake is still a win. A simple sentence you say bravely is better than a perfect sentence you keep inside. So when a word is missing, keep going — use a simple word, make a smart guess, let the sentence move. Brave English beats perfect English.
